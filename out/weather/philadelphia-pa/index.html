About Philadelphia Weather

Philadelphia has a humid subtropical climate with hot, humid summers and cool to cold winters. Located in southeastern Pennsylvania, the city experiences four distinct seasons with moderate precipitation throughout the year.

Summer highs average in the mid-80s°F (29°C) with high humidity, while winter temperatures typically range from the 20s to 40s°F (-7 to 4°C). The city receives about 42 inches of precipitation annually, including snow in winter months.

Philadelphia's weather is influenced by both continental and maritime air masses, creating variable conditions. The city can experience nor'easters, thunderstorms, and occasional severe weather. Spring and fall provide the most comfortable weather with mild temperatures and lower humidity.
